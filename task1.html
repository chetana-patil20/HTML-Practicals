Classic Beef Burger

Serves: 2 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 10 min

Ingredients

  1. 500 g ground beef (80/20 blend) — makes 2 patties
  2. Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  3. 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  4. 2 slices cheddar cheese
  5. 2 burger buns
  6. 1 tbsp butter (for toasting buns)
  7. Lettuce leaves
  8. 1 tomato, sliced
  9. Onion slices (red or white)
  10. Pickles
  11. Ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise

Instructions

  1. Divide the ground beef into two equal portions and gently form into patties slightly wider than the buns. Do not overwork the meat.
  2. Season both sides with salt and pepper and add Worcestershire if using.
  3. Preheat a skillet or grill to medium-high and oil lightly. Cook the patties 3–4 minutes per side for medium, adjusting time for desired doneness.
  4. Place a slice of cheddar on each patty during the last minute of cooking and cover to melt.
  5. Spread butter on the bun cut sides and toast in a pan until golden.
  6. Assemble: spread sauces on buns, add lettuce, tomato, cheesy patty, onion, and pickles. Top and serve immediately.
  7. Optional: add bacon, avocado, or swap beef for a turkey or vegetarian patty.
