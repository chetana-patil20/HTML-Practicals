Classic Margherita Pizza

Serves: 2–3 | Prep: 20 min (+1–2 hr dough rise) | Cook: 8–12 min

Ingredients

  1. For the dough:
    1. 300 g all-purpose flour
    2. 1 tsp instant yeast
    3. 1 tsp salt
    4. 1 tbsp olive oil
    5. 180 ml warm water
  2. 200 g canned crushed tomatoes or pizza sauce
  3. 1–2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  4. 200 g fresh mozzarella, torn or sliced
  5. Fresh basil leaves
  6. 1–2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  7. Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  8. Semolina or cornmeal for dusting (optional)

Instructions

  1. Make the dough: mix flour, yeast, and salt in a bowl. Add warm water and olive oil, stir until a shaggy dough forms, then knead 8–10 minutes until smooth. Cover and let rise until doubled (1–2 hours). (Or use store-bought dough.)
  2. Preheat the oven to 475–500°F (245–260°C). If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat; otherwise use a baking sheet, lightly dusted with semolina or cornmeal.
  3. Prepare the sauce: season crushed tomatoes with a pinch of salt, pepper, and the minced garlic if using. Keep it simple—no need to cook ahead.
  4. Shape the dough: divide dough into portions, press or roll into a 10–12 inch round on a floured surface, leaving a slightly thicker edge for the crust.
  5. Assemble: transfer the shaped dough to a pizza peel or baking sheet. Spread a thin layer of sauce over the base, leaving the edge free. Scatter torn mozzarella evenly and drizzle with a little olive oil.
  6. Bake: slide the pizza onto the preheated stone or put the baking sheet in the oven. Bake 8–12 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  7. Finish: remove from oven, scatter fresh basil leaves, drizzle with a little extra-virgin olive oil, slice, and serve immediately.
  8. Variations: add pepperoni, roasted vegetables, or swap mozzarella for burrata after baking for a richer topping.
